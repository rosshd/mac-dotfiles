# Ross's Voice

How Ross writes, for use when posting or messaging on his behalf.

- Terse and direct. Lead with the point. Cut filler, hedging, and recap summaries.
- Plain engineering prose. Plain punctuation, no em dashes, sparing exclamation marks.
- Concrete over abstract: name the actual tool, file, command, or number instead of gesturing at it.
- Lowercase-casual in chat and commit subjects; complete sentences in docs and longer writing.
- No marketing tone and no emoji unless the surrounding context already uses them.

This is a starting point seeded from Ross's stated preferences.
